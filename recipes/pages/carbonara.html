Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. Itâ€™s creamy, savory, and rich without the use of heavy cream.

The heat of the pasta cooks the eggs gently, creating a silky sauce that clings to each strand of spaghetti.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces spaghetti
  • 4 ounces pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted boiling water according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add pancetta and cook until crispy. Stir in minced garlic for 1 minute, then remove from heat.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together eggs, Parmesan, and black pepper.
  4. Quickly add the hot drained spaghetti to the skillet with pancetta. Toss to combine.
  5. Remove the pan from heat and immediately pour in the egg-cheese mixture, tossing quickly to prevent scrambling. Add reserved pasta water as needed to make a creamy sauce.
  6. Adjust seasoning with extra salt and pepper if needed.
  7. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan sprinkled on top.
